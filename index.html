Essential Accessories for Better Video on Your DSLR or Mirrorless Camera

Videos that are coming out of today’s DSLRs and mirrorless cameras are phenomenal and in many cases can compete with the big boys of cinema cameras. However, there are many ways to get even better video out of your camera.

Essential Accessories

Tripods

The first accessory you should look into getting is a tripod, or at the very least a monopod.At the beginning of my career, I worked with a lower cost tripod and regretted it.

Rating 4.5

Microphone

The VideoMic Pro from Rode allows you to capture significantly cleaner and clearer audio than the internal mic on your camera.When your audio needs to be perfect, hire a professional.

Rating 4.5

LED Video Light

A very bright bi-color light that will work as a key or background light. This light can match up with a large range of light sources from the sun to 3200K bulbs.The needs of lighting vary wildly.

Rating 4.5

Lenses

Your selection of lenses will make a big impact on the quality of your image. The first consideration you will need to make when selecting lenses is which focal lengths you will need.

Rating 4.5

Filters

Filters can help you get creative looks and they can also allow you to shoot in less than ideal settings.The first filter you should get is a variable ND filter.Rings made of brass are of high quality and much better than aluminum or plastic.

Rating 4.5

Stabilizer

Once you have a little bit of budget for camera movement, my recommendation is to first get a slider.Some people get sliders and they no longer have any locked down shots

Rating 4.5

Grip and Gaff Gear

Modifying lighting to get a more pleasing image is one of the key talents of great gaffers and cinematographers.A set of diffusion will help you soften your lights.

Rating 4.5

External Monitor/Recorder

While the codecs that DSLRs and mirrorless cameras record in are getting better, they are still usually very compressed and don’t hold up nearly as well to image manipulation in post as their cinema brethren.
